Haw Par Villa is a unique outdoor cultural theme park built in 1937 by Aw Boon Haw. It features over 1,000 statues and 150 dioramas depicting Chinese mythology, including Journey to the West. The Ten Courts of Hell exhibit teaches Chinese morality with vivid displays. The park is free to enter, making it accessible for all. It’s ideal for history buffs and those seeking quirky attractions. The kitschy yet charming vibe appeals to all ages. Guided tours at 9:30 AM provide deeper insights. The park is accessible via Haw Par Villa MRT. The lush grounds are perfect for leisurely exploration. The park is a must-visit for its cultural uniqueness. Photography is encouraged for its colorful displays. The park is less crowded than other attractions. It’s a fun, educational stop for families. Nearby dining options are limited, so plan ahead. The park reflects Singapore’s multicultural heritage.
**Location**: 262 Pasir Panjang Road, Singapore 118628
**Timings**: 9:00 AM–7:00 PM
**Entry Fee**: Free; guided tours may have a fee
**Tip**: Visit the Ten Courts of Hell for a memorable cultural lesson.
